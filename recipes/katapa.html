The Amazing Ifisashi Recipe:Zambian Food

Katapa or cassava leaves are a popular vegetable in Zambian cuisine and can be prepared in various ways. In this recipe, we will show you how to cook Katapa cassava leaves with the rich and nutty flavor of groundnuts. This traditional Zambian recipe pairs well with nshima and other protein dishes.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of pounded katapa cassava leaves
  • 2-3 tablespoons of groundnuts powder
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Water

Steps

  1. Begin by thoroughly washing the fresh katapa cassava leaves.
  2. Pound or grind the leaves using a mortar and pestle called “Kabende and Kamunsi” until they are finely broken down. Be careful not to grind them too finely.
  3. Alternatively, you can use a blender, but ensure that the leaves do not become overly fine.
  4. Place the pounded katapa cassava leaves into a pot and add water just above the level of the leaves. Start boiling.
  5. Boil the leaves for about 2-3 hours or until they become tender, making sure to add more water if needed.
  6. Once the leaves are tender, add the chopped tomatoes, onions, groundnuts powder, and salt to the pot. Mix well.
  7. Allow the dish to simmer for about 40 minutes on medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.
  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  9. Serve the Katapa with nshima and a protein dish such as dry fish stew.
